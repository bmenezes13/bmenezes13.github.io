What is D&D?

In the simplest of terms, D&D, or Dungeons and Dragons is a tabletop RPG (role-playing game), and it's known as the first fantasy role-playing game in history, having been created by Gary Gygax and Dave Arneson in 1974. In a mostly story-driven adventure, players assume the roles of fictional characters exploring an entire fantasy world full of dungeons, monsters, and of course, dragons, as they are presented by a Dungeon Master (DM).

Ability Scores

When looking at a character sheet, you'll find six very prominent key words: Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma. You'll also be hearing these words come up a lot during game sessions, whether it's when you or a party member are performing an action, or when the dungeon master needs everyone to check for something. Each of these abilities have a number in range from 1 to 20, which measures the magnitude of that ability.

Ability Checks and Modifiers

During a game session, your DM may ask you to perform an ability check, naming one of the six to use. When that happens you'll need to keep in mind your modifier for that ability. A modifier is measured from -5 to +30, and is determined by subtracting 10 from the ability score, then dividing by 2 (rounded down). For example, an ability score of 14 would have a modifier of +2, while a score of 2 would have a modifier of -4. When an ability check is performed, a player will roll a d20 (20-sided die) and add their ability's modifier to the roll, determining your chance of success. This will be contested with a difficulty class (DC) preset by the DM before the check is called for.

Advantage and Disadvantage

Sometimes you'll be asked to make a check with advantage or disadvantage. When this happens, you'll roll the d20 twice instead of once, taking the higher of the two rolls when using advantage, and lower when using disadvantage. This can be caused by a spell effect, a class ability, or even for role-playing reasons within the story of the game

Skills, Saving Throws, and Proficiency Bonus

Each of the six abilities cover a wide range of skills, which are usually measured with the same modifiers as their 'parent' ability. There will also be cases in which you'll be marked as proficient in one or more of these skills and will be given a proficiency bonus from +2 to +6, depending on your character level, which can be added to the check you make for that skill. These bonuses can also be added to saving throws, which are rolls made against certain threats in-game. These threats can come from traps, spells, poison, etc. When choosing your class, you'll be given proficiency in saving throws for two different abilities relating to your character.
